# v0.12.25

## New Features 🎉

* **feat(agentrun): cloud to local coding agent support** ([#3621](https://github.com/pluralsh/console/pull/3621)) by @floreks

  > Support for a cloud-to-local coding agent has been introduced, allowing users to upload session data, screen recordings, and patch files directly from their local environment to the Plural Console. This enhancement streamlines the development workflow by enabling seamless integration of local coding activities with cloud-based operations, thereby improving efficiency and collaboration for teams managing Kubernetes deployments.

* **feat: refactor kustomize renderer** ([#3652](https://github.com/pluralsh/console/pull/3652)) by @zreigz

  > The kustomize renderer has been refactored to enhance its functionality and streamline the rendering process. This update introduces the use of the `krusty` library, allowing for improved handling of Helm configurations and better integration with Kustomize features. As a result, users can expect more efficient and flexible management of Kubernetes manifests, particularly when utilizing Helm alongside Kustomize.

* **feat: target specific gvk** ([#3656](https://github.com/pluralsh/console/pull/3656)) by @zreigz

  > The deployment operator now supports targeting specific Group-Version-Kind (GVK) combinations for custom health scripts, allowing users to define and manage health checks for different Kubernetes resource types independently. This enhancement improves the flexibility and granularity of health monitoring, enabling more tailored responses based on the specific characteristics of each resource. As a result, users can ensure that their health checks are more relevant and effective for diverse workloads within their Kubernetes clusters.

* **Implement workbench model selection** ([#3679](https://github.com/pluralsh/console/pull/3679)) by @michaeljguarino

  > The workbench model selection feature has been implemented, allowing users to choose specific AI models for their workbench jobs. This enhancement streamlines the process of selecting and configuring AI providers, improving the flexibility and customization of AI interactions within the platform. Users can now tailor their workflows more effectively by selecting the most suitable models for their tasks.
